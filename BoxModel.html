Gautam Buddha, also known as Siddhartha Gautama, was a spiritual leader, philosopher, and founder of Buddhism. His teachings and philosophy have influenced millions of people across the world for over two millennia. Here’s a brief overview of his life and teachings:

Birth: Siddhartha Gautama was born around 563 BCE (dates may vary) in Lumbini, which is now in Nepal. He belonged to the Shakya clan, and his father, King Śuddhodana, was a ruler of the small kingdom of Kapilavastu.

Luxury and Isolation: Siddhartha grew up in luxury and was shielded by his father from the hardships of life. His father ensured he remained ignorant of pain, suffering, and death, hoping Siddhartha would follow in his footsteps as a ruler. Marriage: He married Princess Yasodhara, and they had a son named Rahula.
